About Me


I am a Software Developer at NumFOCUS and an Adjunct Instructor at Stark State College. At NumFOCUS, my work supports the development of the SunPy Python library for the analysis of solar physics data. My teaching experience includes introductory physics and astronomy labs/courses that have been taught both in-person and online.

As a graduate student at the University of Missouri-St. Louis, my dissertation research analyzed young stars with circumstellar disks in the open cluster IC 1805 using visual spectroscopic data and archival infrared photometry and how this cluster has influenced the evolution of the larger W4 HII region. I am also interested in physics education, in particular, how relativistic concepts can be integrated into introductory physics curricula and laboratory experiments can be conducted by students outside the classroom.
