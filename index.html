About Me


I am an Aerospace Engineer at HX5, LLC., supporting the Power and Propulsion Element mission design team at the NASA Glenn Research Center. Our work simulating and verifying the low-thrust trajectory design of the first Gateway lunar space station component involves running high-fidelity simulations using the Copernicus Trajectory Design and Optimization System as well as writing/testing custom analysis algorithms in the Python programming language. Previously, I have worked as a software developer supporting the SunPy solar physics analysis package, adjunct instructor teaching in-person and online physics and astronomy courses, intern at the NASA Glenn Research Center, and graduate researcher analyzing the evolution of young stars in the Heart Nebula.

My work as a NASA intern on interstellar lightsails has been recognized by the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics. I am also interested in physics education, in particular, how relativistic concepts can be integrated into introductory physics curricula and laboratory experiments can be conducted outside the classroom. I hold Bachelor of Science degrees in both Physics and Mathematics, Master's of Science in Physics, and a PhD in Physics.
